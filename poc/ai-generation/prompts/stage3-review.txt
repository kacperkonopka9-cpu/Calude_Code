### 2. COMPLIANCE VALIDATION (Ulga B+R Criteria)

Verify ALL THREE mandatory criteria are explicitly addressed:

**a) CELOWOŚĆ (Purposefulness):**
□ Is there explicit mention of "celowość" or "systematyczne prace badawcze"?

□ Is the R&D goal to expand knowledge or create innovation?
□ **Location:** Should appear in Section 1 (Cel projektu) or Section 3 (Nowatorskość)

**b) ELEMENT TWÓRCZY (Creative Element):**
□ Is there explicit mention of "element twórczy" or "oryginalne rozwiązania"?
□ Are creative, non-obvious solutions described?

□ **Location:** Must appear in Section 3 (Nowatorskość i innowacyjność)

**If missing:** Add explicit statement in Section 3.

**c) NOWA WIEDZA (New Knowledge):**
□ Is there explicit mention of "nowa wiedza" or "rozwój kompetencji"?
□ Is new knowledge for the organization described?
□ Are new methods, technologies, or capabilities mentioned?
□ **Location:** Must appear in Section 3 and Section 6 (Rezultaty projektu)

**If missing:** Add explicit statement in Section 3 and/or Section 6.

**CRITICAL:** The summary paragraph at the end MUST reference all three criteria explicitly.

### 3. COHERENCE & FLOW CHECK

Verify logical flow and consistency across sections:

**Cross-section consistency:**
□ Research problems (Section 5) align with methodology (Section 4)
□ Expected results (Section 6) align with project goals (Section 1)
□ Timeline (Section 8) is consistent with project description (Section 2)
□ Innovation claims (Section 3) are supported by research problems (Section 5)

**Narrative flow:**
□ Sections build on each other logically
□ No contradictions between sections
□ Terminology is consistent throughout
□ Transitions between sections are smooth

**If coherence issues found:**
➤ Adjust phrasing to improve alignment
➤ Add transitional sentences between sections if needed
➤ Ensure consistent terminology

---

### 4. GRAMMAR & POLISH LANGUAGE QUALITY

Check for language quality issues:

**Grammar:**
□ Correct verb conjugation and noun declension
□ Proper use of cases (nominative, genitive, accusative, etc.)
□ Subject-verb agreement
□ Correct use of tenses (past, present, future)

**Professional tone:**
□ Formal, professional language appropriate for tax authority
□ Technical terminology used correctly
□ No colloquialisms or informal language
□ Clear and unambiguous phrasing

**Common issues to fix:**
- Anglicisms (e.g., "planować" not "schedulować")
- Overly complex sentences (break into shorter sentences)
- Passive voice overuse (use active voice when clearer)
- Repetitive phrasing (vary sentence structure)
